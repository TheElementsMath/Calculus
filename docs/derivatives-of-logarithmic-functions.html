3.6 Derivatives of Logarithmic Functions

Logarithmic functions are differentiable and play a central role in calculus, especially when working with:

  • growth and decay models
  • rates of change
  • inverse functions
  • complicated products and powers
  • exponential relationships

Two key logarithmic forms appear in calculus:

  • General logarithm: \(y = \log_b x\)
  • Natural logarithm: \(y = \ln x\) (base \(e\))

Natural logarithms are especially important because their derivative has the simplest form.

Theorem 3.10 \[ \frac{d}{dx}(\log_b x) = \frac{1}{x \ln b} \]

\[ \frac{d}{dx}(\ln x) = \frac{1}{x} \]

This simplicity is one of the main reasons base \(e\) is fundamental in calculus.

Theorem 3.11 \[ \frac{d}{dx}(\ln u) = \frac{1}{u}\frac{du}{dx} \]

or equivalently,

\[ \frac{d}{dx}\big[\ln(f(x))\big] = \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)} \]

Example 3.35 Chain Rule with Natural Log

Find the derivative of \[ y = \ln(x^3 + 1) \]

Solution

Let \(u = x^3 + 1\). Then:

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{u} \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{x^3+1} (3x^2) = \frac{3x^2}{x^3+1} \]

Example 3.36 Log of a Trig Function

Differentiate: \[ y = \ln(\sin x) \]

Solution

\[ y' = \frac{1}{\sin x}(\cos x) = \cot x. \]

Example 3.37 Logarithm as Inner Function

Differentiate \[ f(x) = \sqrt{\ln x} \]

Solution

\[ f'(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\ln x}} \cdot \frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{2x\sqrt{\ln x}} \]

Example 3.38 Base-10 Logarithm

Differentiate \[ f(x) = \log_{10}(2 + \sin x) \]

Solution

\[ f'(x) = \frac{1}{(2+\sin x)\ln 10} \cdot \cos x = \frac{\cos x}{(2+\sin x)\ln 10}. \]

Example 3.39 Absolute Value

Differentiate \[ f(x) = \ln|x| \]

Solution

\[ f'(x) = \frac{1}{x}, \quad x \ne 0 \]

3.6.1 Logarithmic Differentiation

Logarithmic differentiation is a technique used when functions involve:

  • products
  • quotients
  • powers with variable exponents
  • complicated expressions

Steps in Logarithmic Differentiation

  1. Take natural logarithms of both sides
  2. Use laws of logarithms to simplify
  3. Differentiate implicitly
  4. Solve for \(\frac{dy}{dx}\)
  5. Substitute back for \(y\)

Example 3.40 Product, Quotient, and Powers

Find \(y^{\prime}\) if \[ y = \frac{x^{3/4}(x^2+1)^{1/2}}{(3x+2)^5}. \]

Solution:

Take logs: \[ \ln y = \frac{3}{4}\ln x + \frac{1}{2}\ln(x^2+1) - 5\ln(3x+2) \]

Differentiate: \[ \frac{1}{y}y' = \frac{3}{4x} + \frac{x}{x^2+1} - \frac{15}{3x+2} \]

Solve: \[ y' = y\left(\frac{3}{4x} + \frac{x}{x^2+1} - \frac{15}{3x+2}\right) \]

Substitute original \(y\): \[ y' = \frac{x^{3/4}(x^2+1)^{1/2}}{(3x+2)^5}\left(\frac{3}{4x} + \frac{x}{x^2+1} - \frac{15}{3x+2}\right) \]

Example 3.41 Variable Base and Variable Exponent

Differentiate \[ y = x^x \]

Solution

\[ \ln y = x\ln x \]

Differentiate: \[ \frac{1}{y}y' = \ln x + 1 \] So, \[ y' = x^x(\ln x + 1) \]

3.6.2 Putting It All Together

Logarithmic derivatives unify many differentiation rules:

  • Product Rule
  • Quotient Rule
  • Power Rule
  • Chain Rule
  • Exponential differentiation
  • Implicit differentiation

into one powerful method. They allow us to differentiate functions that would otherwise require long, error-prone algebra.

3.6.2.1 Conceptual Takeaways

  • Natural logarithms are fundamental because \(\frac{d}{dx}(\ln x) = \frac{1}{x}\)
  • Logarithms convert multiplication into addition
  • Logarithms convert powers into products
  • Logarithmic differentiation simplifies structure before differentiation
  • Absolute values are required for logarithms of expressions that may be negative
  • Differentiation becomes algebraically easier after taking logs
  • Many “hard-looking” derivatives become simple after log transformation

3.6.2.2 Skills You Should Be Able to Do

After this section, you should be able to:

  • Differentiate \(\ln x\), \(\log_b x\), and \(\ln(f(x))\)
  • Apply the Chain Rule with logarithms
  • Differentiate \(\ln|x|\)
  • Use logarithmic identities to simplify derivatives
  • Differentiate functions of the form \(f(x)^{g(x)}\)
  • Recognize when logarithmic differentiation is the best method
  • Combine logarithmic differentiation with implicit differentiation
