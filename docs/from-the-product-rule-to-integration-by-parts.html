7.2 From the Product Rule to Integration by Parts

The Product Rule for differentiation states: \[ \frac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)] = f(x)g'(x) + g(x)f'(x) \]

Rewriting this in integral form: \[ \int \big(f(x)g'(x) + g(x)f'(x)\big)\,dx = f(x)g(x) \]

Rearranging: \[ \int f(x)g'(x)\,dx = f(x)g(x) - \int g(x)f'(x)\,dx \]

7.2.1 Integration by Parts Formula

Let:

  • \(u = f(x)\)
  • \(dv = g'(x)\,dx\)
  • \(du = f'(x)\,dx\)
  • \(v = g(x)\)

Then: \[ \int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du. \]

Strategy Pattern:

Always organize your work as: \[\begin{align*} u &= _______ &dv &= _______\\ du &= _______ &v &= _______\\ \end{align*}\]

7.2.2 Choosing \(u\) and \(dv\)

General Principle:

Choose:

  • \(u\): the function that becomes simpler when differentiated
  • \(dv\): the function that is easy to integrate

Common heuristic (LIATE rule):

Logarithmic → Inverse trig → Algebraic → Trig → Exponential

Choose \(u\) from the earliest category in this list.

Example 7.1 Integrate \[\int x \sin(x) \; dx\]

Solution: \[\begin{align*} u &= x &dv &= \sin(x) dx\\ du &= dx &v &= -\cos(x)\\ \end{align*}\]

Then, \[\begin{align*} \int x \sin(x) \; dx &= uv - \int v \; du\\ &= -x\cos(x) + \int \cos(x) \; dx\\ &= -x\cos(x) + \sin(x) + C \end{align*}\]

Example 7.2 Integrate \[\int \ln(x) \; dx\]

Solution: \[\begin{align*} u &= \ln x &dv &= dx\\ du &= 1/x dx &v &= x\\ \end{align*}\]

Then, \[\begin{align*} \int \ln(x) \; dx &= uv - \int v \; du\\ &= x\ln(x) - \int 1 \; dx\\ &= x\ln(x) - x + C \end{align*}\]

Example 7.3 Integrate \[\int \t^2 e^t \; dt\]

Solution: \[\begin{align*} u &= t^2 &dv &= e^t dt\\ du &= 2t dt &v &= e^t\\ \end{align*}\]

Then, \[\begin{align*} \int t^2 e^t \; dt &= uv - \int v \; du\\ &= t^2 e^t - 2\int te^t\; dt \end{align*}\]

Second iteration \[\begin{align*} u &= t &dv &= e^t dt\\ du &= dt &v &= e^t\\ \end{align*}\] Then, \[\begin{align*} \int t e^t \; dt &= uv - \int v \; du\\ &= t e^t - \int e^t\; dt\\ &= t e^t - e^t \end{align*}\] Therefore, \[\int t^2 e^t \; dt = t^2 e^t - 2\int te^t\; dt =t^2 e^t - 2(t e^t - e^t) + C.\]

Example 7.4 Integrate \[\int e^x \sin(x) \; dx\]

Solution: \[\begin{align*} u &= e^x &dv &= \sin(x) dx\\ du &= e^x dx &v &= -\cos(x)\\ \end{align*}\]

Then, \[\begin{align*} \int e^x \sin(x) \; dx &= uv - \int v \; du\\ &= -e^x\cos(x) + \int e^x \cos(x) \; dx \end{align*}\]

Second pass: \[\begin{align*} u &= e^x &dv &= \cos(x) dx\\ du &= e^x dx &v &= \sin(x)\\ \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} \int e^x \cos(x) \; dx &= uv - \int v \; du\\ &= e^x\sin(x) - \int e^x \sin(x) \; dx \end{align*}\]

Put that together: \[\begin{align*} \int e^x \sin(x) \; dx &= -e^x\cos(x) + e^x\sin(x) - \int e^x \sin(x) \; dx \\ 2\int e^x \sin(x) \; dx &= -e^x\cos(x) + e^x\sin(x) \\ \int e^x \sin(x) \; dx &= \dfrac{-e^x\cos(x) + e^x\sin(x)}{2} \end{align*}\]

Therefore, \[\int e^x \sin(x) \; dx &= \dfrac{-e^x\cos(x) + e^x\sin(x)}{2} + C.\]

Definite integrals work the same way: \[\int_{{\,a}}^{{\,b}}{{u\,dv}} = \left. {uv} \right|_a^b - \int_{{\,a}}^{{\,b}}{{v\,du}}\]

Example 7.5 Integrate \[\int_{{\, - 1}}^{{\,2}}{{x{{\bf{e}}^{6x}}\,dx}}\]

Solution: Using integration by parts:

\[\begin{align*} \int_{{\, - 1}}^{{\,2}}{{x{{\bf{e}}^{6x}}\,dx}} & = \left. {\frac{x}{6}{{\bf{e}}^{6x}}} \right|_{ - 1}^2 - \frac{1}{6}\int_{{\, - 1}}^{{\,2}}{{{{\bf{e}}^{6x}}\,dx}}\\ & = \left. {\frac{x}{6}{{\bf{e}}^{6x}}} \right|_{ - 1}^2 - \left. {\frac{1}{{36}}{{\bf{e}}^{6x}}} \right|_{ - 1}^2\\ & = \frac{{11}}{{36}}{{\bf{e}}^{12}} + \frac{7}{{36}}{{\bf{e}}^{ - 6}}. \end{align*}\]

Example 7.6 Integrate \[\int_{{\, 0}}^{{\,1}} \tan^{-1}(x) \; dx.\]

Solution: Using IBP, \[\begin{align*} \int_{{\, 0}}^{{\,1}} \tan^{-1}(x) \; dx &= uv - \int v \; du\\ &= \left. x \tan^{-1}(x) \right|_{ 0}^1 - \int_{{\, 0}}^{{\,1}} \frac{x}{1 + x^2} \,dx\\ &= \frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{1}{2} \ln(2).\\ \end{align*}\]

7.2.2.1 Conceptual Takeaways

  • Integration by parts is the inverse of the Product Rule
  • It is a structure-based method, not mechanical
  • The goal is always simplification
  • It transfers differentiation from one function to another
  • Some integrals require repeated application
  • Some integrals create self-referential equations
  • It generates reduction formulas
  • It connects derivative structure to integral structure

7.2.2.2 Skills You Should Be Able To Do

After this section, you should be able to:

  • Identify when integration by parts is appropriate
  • Choose effective \(u\) and \(dv\)
  • Apply the formula correctly
  • Perform repeated integration by parts
  • Solve cyclic integrals algebraically
  • Evaluate definite integrals by parts
  • Build reduction formulas
  • Combine with substitution
  • Verify answers by differentiation
  • Recognize poor choices of \(u\) and \(dv\)
  • Use structural reasoning, not memorization
